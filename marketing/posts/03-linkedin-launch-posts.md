# LinkedIn launch posts — 3 sets

> LinkedIn rewards longer-form (3–6 short paragraphs), narrative-led, build-in-public posts. Hooks on lines 1–2 — anything past line 3 is hidden behind "see more" until clicked.
> Each set = 3 posts to ship across launch week, designed as a sequence.
> Always: first comment from your own account with the link (LinkedIn de-prioritises posts with links in the body).

## SET B — Practical / advice-led

**Why this set:** less personal, more "I built X, here's the takeaway you can use." Suits LinkedIn audiences that scroll for tactical content. Slower to convert customers but builds founder authority for future hiring / partnerships / fundraising.

### B1 — "5 things I'd tell 2014 me"

**Hook:**
> 5 things I'd tell my younger self running a cabinet shop:

**Body:**

> 1. Quote in minutes, not hours. The faster you quote, the more you win. The slower you quote, the more your competitors win.
>
> 2. The schedule isn't a calendar — it's a daily forecast. If you can't see two weeks of work at a glance, you're flying blind on cash flow.
>
> 3. Stock tracking only works if it's a one-click action *as you cut*. Anything else and you'll abandon it within a month.
>
> 4. The most expensive jobs are the ones you re-quote three times. Templates aren't laziness — they're how you stop wasting estimating time.
>
> 5. The notebook is a security blanket. Replace it the moment you can — because the day someone needs to cover for you, the notebook is useless.
>
> Built ProCabinet.App on each of these — link in the comments.

### B2 — Shop economics post

**Hook:**
> Sheet waste costs the average cabinet shop about £60–£120 per kitchen.

**Body:**

> Multiply by 30 kitchens a year and that's a holiday.
>
> Two interventions, both quick wins:
>
> First — actually plan your sheet layout instead of cutting "as you go". A decent optimiser with kerf + grain awareness routinely cuts waste by 8–15%.
>
> Second — track sheet stock at the moment of cutting, not at the start of the next job. The sheet you "remembered" buying was sold to the previous job and now you're calling the supplier on a Friday afternoon.
>
> ProCabinet.App does both, in one click. Free to try.

## Posting rules

- Always put the link in **comment 1** from your own account (not in the post body — LinkedIn shadow-suppresses link posts).
- Reply to every comment in the first 6 hours — the comment-engagement window is wider on LinkedIn than IG.
- Tag 2–3 people who'd want to see the post (people you actually know — random tags = mute).
- 3–5 hashtags max, all relevant, in the body. Bury them at the bottom or use sparingly.

## Hashtag set

`#Cabinetmaking #SmallBusiness #BuildInPublic #SaaS #IndieHacker #Joinery #ShopOwner #Cabinetry`

## When to post

- LinkedIn algorithm favours **Tuesday–Thursday, 7–9 AM local**.
- Avoid Mondays before 10 AM (low engagement) and Fridays after 3 PM (engagement cliff).
- For a global English audience: 7 AM UK time = covers UK + early US East coast.
