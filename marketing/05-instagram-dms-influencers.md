## Rules of engagement

1. **Personalise the opener.** If your first 2 lines could be sent to anyone, you've already lost.
2. **Reference real content.** Watch their last 2 posts. Name one specific thing. Lazy compliments ("great content!") are obvious.
3. **State the offer clearly in 1 sentence.** "Free lifetime account in exchange for an honest review within 30 days."
4. **Make the no-obligation clause loud.** "If you don't like it, keep the account, no review needed." Reduces resistance massively.
5. **Set a time limit.** "Within 30 days" creates a soft deadline without pressure. After 30 days the account quietly converts to standard pricing.
6. **Don't gift to anyone you haven't actually watched.** The first sentence of the review post will reveal you didn't.
7. **Track each gifted spot** in your CRM with: handle, audience size, tier (macro/mid/micro), gifted date, content shipped (y/n), URL of resulting post, signups attributed via UTM.
8. **One follow-up only**, 5 days later, even shorter:
   > {name} — friendly bump on the free lifetime offer in case the DM got buried. Spots are filling up. Either way, the {latest post} is great.

## Per-creator UTM template

Every gifted account should be tracked. When you send the login email, also send them a UTM-tagged link they should use in their video description / IG bio:

```
https://procabinet.app/?utm_source=creator&utm_medium=gift&utm_campaign={handle}
```

Replace `{handle}` with their @ (no spaces, no special chars). e.g.:

```
https://procabinet.app/?utm_source=creator&utm_medium=gift&utm_campaign=woodbywayne
```

That tells you exactly which creator drove which signup.

## How to find 75 cabinetmaker creators in a weekend

1. **YouTube — search:** *"cabinet shop tour" / "small cabinet shop" / "cabinet maker workflow" / "kitchen cabinet build" / "cabinet pricing"*. Sort by upload date, last 12 months. Save channels.
2. **Instagram — hashtags:** #cabinetshop #cabinetmaker #joinery #customkitchens #cabinetbuilder #wardrobemaker. Tap each, sort by Reels (better signal than grid).
3. **TikTok — search:** *"cabinetmaker" / "cabinet shop" / "joinery" / "kitchen install"*. Save the ones that are working shops, not DIY.
4. **Cross-reference:** open one creator's profile and check who they tag / collab with.
5. **The Anatomy List:** every cabinetmaker creator follows a small handful of "elder statesmen" — find one big one, scroll their followers list for shops with engaged accounts.

Spreadsheet columns: handle, platform, audience, last post date, niche fit (1–5), notes for opener, DM sent, replied, gifted, content shipped, URL, signups.

## What "honest review" actually means (set the expectation in the gift email)

When you send the login, include:

> No script. Use the app on a real job for at least a week before reviewing. Tell your audience what works AND what doesn't — the honest stuff converts way better than puff. If something's broken or missing, tell me first and I'll usually fix it within 48 hours. No ad disclosure needed beyond what your platform requires (yes for IG/TikTok if you're in regions that require it).

This single paragraph builds trust and produces vastly better content than a tightly-scripted brief.
