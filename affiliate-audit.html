Strategic advice — what to change

1. Flip the effort: recruitment is the product, the funnel is plumbing.
Your 90-day plan spends weeks 1–6 building and only softly recruits. Invert it: run outbound continuously from week 2. Concretely, mine where cabinet-software buyers already look:
  • Listicles: Google intitle:("best" OR "top") intitle:"cabinet software" (also "cutlist software", "woodworking business software") — writers of these already monetise with affiliate links.
  • Competitor mentions/reviews: creators covering CutList Plus, Mozaik, Cabinet Vision, SketchList — pitch a review + program invite + free access.
  • Competitor affiliates: if you ever get Ahrefs access, filter competitor backlinks for ?ref=/?via= tags to extract their affiliate lists.
  • Target: 10 personalised outreach emails/week, 2 follow-ups each (follow-ups produce ~half of all replies). Rewardful's free outreach generator drafts these.
2. Expect the Pareto shape and plan for it. 1–5% of affiliates will drive most revenue. Your job after launch is finding your 2–3 real performers and treating them like partners: beta access, a call, custom assets, maybe a private higher-rate campaign in Rewardful later. Your tiering table (§3) is good — but relevance beats reach: a 3k-follower one-man-band joiner channel can outperform a 100k generic woodworking channel.
3. Lock the two undefined rules before launch. Attribution model (recommend last-touch) and a payout threshold (your $50 is fine) go in the T&Cs and the affiliate FAQ. No retroactive changes ever — existing referrals keep original terms if you adjust rates later.
4. Keep 30%/12mo public — don't launch with variants. Your instinct in §4 is right: one clean public rate. Rewardful's 2,500-campaign analysis shows higher commissions don't buy better results; product ease-of-pitch does. Your live-quote-link demo is the pitch — lead every outreach with it.
Decided 2026-07-10 — Founding creator cohort: lifetime commission, capped at 10. The first creators get 25–30% recurring lifetime (gross) as a private Rewardful campaign; the public program stays 30%/12mo. Rationale: at ~15-month average customer life, lifetime costs only ~$31 more per customer than the 12-month cap ($157 vs $126) and only gets expensive if retention improves — exactly when it's affordable. The "after payment fees" idea was dropped: netting out ~3% Stripe fees saves ~$0.09/customer/mo but wrecks the headline and creator trust — if margin worries, use 25% lifetime gross instead of 30% net. Grandfathering is clean (Rewardful keeps original terms on existing referrals); add a good-standing clause to the T&Cs. The lifetime hook is the pitch for the Phase 2 warm invites.
5. Add the two free passive channels the plan misses. (a) In-app affiliate invite for existing customers at a satisfaction milestone; (b) email-signature mention + a line in the Resend welcome flow you already run. Both compound at zero cost.

Revised priority order (next 4 weeks)

  1. Week 1: Rewardful signup + Stripe connect, set 30%/12mo public + private founding-cohort campaign (25–30% lifetime gross, cap 10), 60-day cookie, last-touch, $50 threshold, 30-day hold. Write T&Cs incl. good-standing clause (Rewardful template). Confirm extended-trial incentive.
